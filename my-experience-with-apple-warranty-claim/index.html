My experience with Apple warranty claim

I got my MacBook Pro in the February of 2013. Until the mid of May 2013, it works great, and all of a sudden, it freezes completely. I have to long press the power button to force it to shut down. When I switch it back on, the whole system is very slow. After much struggling, I tried reinstalling OS X using the recovery partition. After reinstalling, the system becomes faster, but not as fast as when it is new, in fact, not as fast as its last known good state. After a while, the Disk Utility gave me a error message which basically means the hard drive is about to fail. So, I decided to send my Mac back to Apple service centre to let them have a check.

I sent it in on a Wednesday, during the last few hours of their business that day. Upon showing them that error message, the person-in-charge explained all the possible scenario that is currently happening - hard drive failure or software corruption. For the former case, I will be getting a new drive for my Mac, covered by warranty; for the latter case, they will wipe my drive and reinstall OS X (again). He said that my Mac will be evaluated for two days, and they will contact me on Friday, after that, if it is the case of hard drive failure, it will take up to five working days for them to claim a new drive and replace the old one with the new one. This means that I will have nine days without my Mac. Luckily, I have a Chromebook with me that time. So at least I am not without a machine.

Frankly, I felt that the upper bound of the time span that they predicted is reasonable. First of all, that service centre is not directly owned by Apple - it is another computer company which is granted the “Apple Premium Service Provider” status. Also, let’s face it, warranty claims are always tedious. For example, once my sister’s Logitech mouse broke down and she sent it for warranty claim. It took them two months to replace a new one. So, compare to two months, nine days seemed very reasonable.

Well, it took them less than two days to evaluate my Mac - they called back the next day, that is, on Thursday, and confirmed that my drive was failing. I thought that I will be getting my Mac back one day earlier, that is, on the next Thursday. I was wrong. They made a phone call to be to collect on Monday.

End of story. I am so satisfied.
